# Understanding the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion

The Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE) is one of the most valuable tax benefits available to U.S. citizens living abroad. It allows eligible expats to exclude a significant portion of their foreign-earned income from U.S. federal taxation.

## Who Qualifies?

To qualify for the FEIE, you must meet one of two tests:

1. **Bona Fide Residence Test**: You must be a bona fide resident of a foreign country for an uninterrupted tax year.
2. **Physical Presence Test**: You must be physically present in a foreign country for at least 330 full days during a 12-month period.

## How Much Can You Exclude?

For the 2024 tax year, the maximum exclusion is $126,500. This amount is adjusted annually for inflation.

## What Income Qualifies?

The FEIE applies to:
- Wages and salaries
- Professional fees
- Bonuses and commissions
- Other compensation for personal services

It does not apply to:
- Investment income
- Rental income
- Pension income
- Capital gains

## Conclusion

The FEIE can significantly reduce your U.S. tax liability while living abroad. However, the rules are complex, and proper planning is essential to maximize your benefits while staying compliant.
