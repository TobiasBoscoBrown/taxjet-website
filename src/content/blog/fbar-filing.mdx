# FBAR Filing Requirements for Expats

The Foreign Bank Account Report (FBAR) is a critical compliance requirement for U.S. citizens and residents with foreign financial accounts.

## What is the FBAR?

The FBAR, officially known as FinCEN Form 114, is used to report foreign financial accounts to the U.S. Department of Treasury.

## Who Must File?

You must file an FBAR if:
- You are a U.S. citizen, resident, or entity
- You have a financial interest in or signature authority over foreign financial accounts
- The aggregate value of those accounts exceeded $10,000 at any point during the calendar year

## What Accounts Must Be Reported?

Reportable accounts include:
- Bank accounts
- Investment accounts
- Securities accounts
- Certain insurance policies
- Certain retirement accounts

## Filing Deadline

The FBAR must be filed electronically by April 15th, with an automatic extension to October 15th.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Failure to file can result in significant penalties:
- Non-willful violations: Up to $10,000 per violation
- Willful violations: Up to $100,000 or 50% of account balance, whichever is greater

## Conclusion

FBAR compliance is serious business. If you have foreign accounts, ensure you understand your reporting requirements and file on time.
