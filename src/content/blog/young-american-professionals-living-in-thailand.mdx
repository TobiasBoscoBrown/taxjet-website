To all the young American professionals living in Thailand: you came last year with the idea of taking a break, maybe traveling a bit, or even working remotely. Months have passed, and your adventure turned into something more permanent. You're loving the food, the culture, and the lifestyle.

Here's the thing. If you're earning income from a U.S. job, or even just living in Thailand long-term, you're officially an expat.

You might not have planned it this way, but as a U.S. citizen living abroad you're now part of a group the IRS refers to as expats, and that comes with some important tax responsibilities. Even though you're living in Thailand, the U.S. tax system still expects you to file.

## So what do you need to do?

First off, don't worry. If you earned income from a U.S.-based job before you decided to stay in Thailand, you've downloaded your W-2 and the IRS still wants its share. The good news: you may be able to exclude some of that income through benefits built for expats, like the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion. You just need to file correctly to take advantage of it.
